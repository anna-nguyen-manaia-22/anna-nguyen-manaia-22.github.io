1. Being blocked on a problem

In this sprint, there were many times I was blocked because I did not understand how to use the functions properly. I would not call them simple problems because they may be simple with an experienced coder but not with a beginner. To solve them, I read the error messages, re-read and re-do them in other ways to see if anything changed. Referring just only one site is not enough for me, I need to google many examples to master how to use it.

2. Elegantly solved a problem

The hardest challenge for me was Gradebook kata as there are many objects, strings, functions that need to put together and it was so confusing. I don't know if I can say I solved it elegantly or not. I tried googling and reading error message but I was still blocked. I also tried to break it into small parts, using pseudocode made it a bit clearer but still did not reach the goal. Luckily, Joseph made the live coaching session and it helped me solve this kata.

3. Reflect on problem solving technique

Pseudocode:

I try to follow the instruction of the challenge and break it into smaller steps. I like this technique and I understand it is really really important but somehow I feel I cannot express the task properly and clearly in words. And it becomes “ too many words” that messes with the main coding so after finishing coding I cleaned it up.

Trying something:

I try it many times and it helps.

Rubber ducky method:

I haven’t tried. First time I learnt about it and I was surprised by how simple and logical it is. It works even with non-coding problems. Definitely, I will try it.

Reading error messages:

They help me to see exactly where the problem is and suggest how to fix it.

Console.logging:

It is very helpful to see whether I am on the right track.

Googling

Yes, I try to google the syntax, method, etc again and again to be sure that I don’t make mistakes because of the wrong use.

Asking your peers for help:

So far I haven’t made a lot of questions for my cohort but I saw many of the questions are the same as mine and I got the answers from those.

Asking coaches for help

I don't hesitate to ask for help but still haven't utilized my chance. In sprint 3 and 4, the live sessions with Joseph were really in time and helped me a lot.

Your process with reflection

Reflection is a good way to improve my skills. After finishing a code, I come back and check it on another day before the due date, think about whether I can do it in a better way. I am trying to make it my habit.
