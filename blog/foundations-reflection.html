Foundation Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

The first new thing for me is to think like a programmer. It is very important as I am switching from a very different background to tech. I need to understand it thoroughly and make it my new habit, especially in problem-solving.

The second is I need to develop a growth mindset in my learning. It plays an important role in determining the outcome of the course and how successful I can be in the future.

The third thing I have learned is how important to work effectively with other people in a coding project is. I will do not work alone but work with other people. Co-operating well helps the job be done smoothly. If I get stuck, there will be someone who can help me solve the problems.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I think personal values can direct every part of my learning. Knowing what are important to me keep me motivated in working to get what I want. Self-awareness is very important. After each sprint, reflecting on what I have done well or not well enough help me improve in the next time. Empathy helps me understand and work well with other people in the cohort.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Before the course, I was wondering how EDA students can become successful developers after such a short time course. I thought that it would teach a huge amount of technical knowledge to students, but in fact, what surprised me the most is that EDA has put so much effort to equip students with human skills. It is really important like the way it is named "core learning". The content is interesting and helpful for any kind of job but especially it was well selected to teach technical students. I used to think that I knew these skills but this is the first time I spent that amount of time learning about it seriously.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging part of core learning for me was exploring my own identity, values and strengths. Understanding myself thoroughly is not easy. It took me quite an amount of time to think and write it down.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Devacademy is spending so much time focusing on core learning because to become a good developer, the core skills/ human skills are the must-have ones. The technical skills are also important but they can be improved when you work and it will be not too hard when you have good human skills.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, I don't think it was a waste of time. It helped me understand clearly what I need to plan and engagage in study.
