1. Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and the consequences of your action.

When I was applying for a new position at a new company, the recruiter asked me to send them a copy of the previous staff ID card as evidence that I was working in the industry at that time. The staff ID card has the ID number that belongs to the company that I was working for so I think I should not reveal it. Aware of this, I discussed with the recruiter about my concern and they accepted the copy of my card with the ID number covered. Then they thanked me for showing professionalism.

2. Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I am from an Asian culture where hardworking, loyalty and family are well respected. Those things are affecting the way I live and work. I am honest and truthful to people around me. I always try my best to do the job I am given. My family is always the most important thing in my life. I am always looking for a work-life balance so that I can have time to take care of my family while doing my favourite job.

I also have good organizational skills which help me to manage my duties at work and at home. English is one of my limitations as I am not an English native speaker but I am working hard to improve it.
