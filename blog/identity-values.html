1. Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and the consequences of your action.

When I was applying for a new position at a new company, the recruiter asked me to send them a copy of the previous staff ID card as evidence that I was working in the industry at that time. The staff ID card has the ID number that belongs to the company that I was working for so I think I should not reveal it. Aware of this, I discussed with the recruiter about my concern and they accepted the copy of my card with the ID number covered. Then they thanked me for showing professionalism.

2. Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I am from an Asian culture where hardworking, loyalty and family are well respected. Those things are affecting the way I live and work. I am honest and truthful to people around me. I always try my best to do the job I am given. My family is always the most important thing in my life. I am always looking for a work-life balance so that I can have time to take care of my family while doing my favourite job.

3. Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development

I can relate to people and adapt to different environments as I used to live in 3 different countries since I was 20. I also have good organizational skills which help me to manage my duties at work and at home. English is one of my limitations as I am not an English native speaker but I am working hard to improve it.

4. Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they intersect with learning obstacles.

I think both of my two strengths can help me in adapt quickly to the new learning environment as well as dealing with learning challenges.

5. Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

In my previous job, in our team, there was a staff who was not trying her best to finish her task on time which affected the other people. Every time someone required her to do the job properly, she just gave a lot of reasons for the delay. I was also trying to talk with her, to understand her circumstance and gave her some suggestions to improve. However, she did not change. Finally, our manager called her to talk and she was changed to another position so that her job would not affect other people. My strategy did not work well in this case but I think if I met a similar person now, I would do the same thing. Communicating to understand others in a team is important. If I cannot help, I would report to the manager.
